Strata vs. Individual Title: What Kajang Property Buyers Should Know

M Legasi | September 25, 2025

Before buying a property in Kajang, it's important to do thorough research. One key point is understanding the difference between a strata title and an individual title. A strata title is typically issued for properties that share common facilities. An individual title means you have full ownership of both the building and the land.

How an Individual Title Property Can Be Better than a Strata Title

Owning a property with an individual title means full ownership of your house and the land it sits on. This offers a level of independence that is hard to match -- you can renovate, extend, or personalise your home without needing approvals from a management body.

Unlike strata properties, you're not bound by communal by-laws that may limit what you can do with your own space. For buyers who value freedom and autonomy, an individual title property is generally more suitable.

You also avoid ongoing strata management fees, and individual title properties tend to enjoy greater privacy and stronger long-term capital appreciation.

While an individual title offers notable benefits, choosing a property shouldn't be based on the title alone. It's equally important to consider other features that match your lifestyle and long-term needs.
